Deep‑Tech Venture Architecture

Building a Scalable Deep‑Tech Venture from Academic R&D

A research team had developed a promising manufacturing process but lacked a clear path from academic experimentation to a scalable product. They needed clarity on technical feasibility, early market validation, and a venture setup capable of turning laboratory results into manufacturable, commercially viable technology.

  • Lean hardware development - stage-gate structure and rapid prototyping to validate technical and product assumptions early
  • Digital manufacturing architecture - systematic control of process windows and parameters to ensure stable, scalable production
  • Root‑cause analysis frameworks - Ishikawa, 5 Whys, and PFMEA to stabilize early‑stage processes
  • Process and data modeling - mapping critical variables to ensure predictable material behavior and repeatability
  • Industry 4.0 readiness - early digital‑twin and data‑capture concepts to prepare for scalable production

Outcome: A structured development approach and stabilized process windows enabled the transition from academic experimentation to a manufacturable hardware prototype within months and under tight budget constraints - creating a clear path toward scalable production and early commercial validation.

Product & Manufacturing Strategy

Defining a Competitive Product and Manufacturing Strategy for Precision Components

A European manufacturer of small, high-precision components was facing increasing cost pressure and declining competitiveness. To enter emerging markets such as AR/VR and automotive head-up displays, the company needed a new production concept, a differentiated product strategy, and clarity on which technologies and materials could enable viable mass production in Europe.

  • Product and market innovation - identifying attractive applications in high-growth, technology-driven markets
  • NPI and equipment translation - adapting proven cross-industry manufacturing technologies for optical components
  • Material innovation - introducing novel functional materials to enable new processing approaches
  • Semi‑automated production concepts - scalable architectures for micro‑optics manufacturing in Europe
  • Technology roadmap definition - structured capability development aligned with long-term product strategy

Outcome: A differentiated product and manufacturing strategy enabled competitive European production of high-precision optical components and positioned the company for successful entry into new growth markets.

Precision Engineering Execution

Engineering an Automated Precision Assembly Line to Reduce Manual Labor

A manufacturer of precision modules relied heavily on manual assembly, limiting throughput, increasing variability, and restricting scalability. To meet growing demand and reduce cost per part, the company needed an integrated automation concept, qualified suppliers, and disciplined engineering execution to combine multiple process steps into a stable, high-yield production line.

  • Automation architecture - integrating cleaning, bonding, coating, and inspection into a unified automated production line
  • Supplier qualification - hands-on evaluation and alignment of equipment partners and component suppliers across Europe and Asia
  • MES–ERP integration - full traceability, structured data capture, and operational transparency across all process steps
  • Operations engineering - optimizing OEE, WIP, and cycle time to ensure stable, high-precision throughput
  • Capability transfer - structured training and documentation to embed new production know-how within the client team

Outcome: A fully integrated automation concept reduced manual handling by 90%, significantly improved yield stability, and lowered cost per part by more than 70% - enabling predictable, high-precision production at scale.

Technology & Innovation Assessment

Evaluating Technology and Innovation Potential for Industrial M&A Targets

An investment team assessing industrial acquisition targets needed a consistent, technology-focused framework to compare companies with different product portfolios, maturity levels, and innovation paths. They required a structured evaluation of technological strength, competitive position, and long-term scalability to support confident, evidence-based investment decisions.

  • Technology evaluation - assessing maturity, scalability, and differentiation of core industrial technologies
  • Innovation roadmap analysis - identifying capability gaps, technical risks, and growth potential
  • Cross‑industry comparison - benchmarking product, technology, and IP portfolios across sectors
  • Market and ecosystem analysis - structured assessment using Porter’s Five Forces and adjacent‑market mapping
  • Decision support - clear scoring frameworks enabling consistent comparison across targets

Outcome: A structured, technology-driven assessment delivered clear scorecards across industrial M&A targets - enabling confident investment decisions grounded in technological maturity, innovation potential, and long-term scalability.
